The operational control plane for production AI agents.

Cost caps, approval gates, workflow-lifecycle policy, and a durable audit log — enforced by a control plane, not hand-coded into every agent.

See it work

A cost regression, caught and rolled back automatically.

A workflow runs on a periodic schedule under a lifecycle policy. When its lifetime cost crosses the configured budget, BoundFlow rolls it back to the last good version — no one paged, no manual intervention.

Production guardrails

Agents are easy to demo and hard to guardrail in production.

The moment an agent runs unattended, you need answers to: what if it loops? What if it spends $50? What if it's about to do something irreversible? Which model should it use, and when should that change? BoundFlow makes those decisions policy, not code you scatter across every agent.

Cost caps

A max_cost_usd runtime cap halts a run the moment its cost crosses budget.

Human approval gates

Park the workflow for a human decision before it takes an irreversible action.

Tool & output limits

Per-tool call limits and token caps stop runaway output and action blowups before they compound.

Pause, cooldown & rollback

Pause a workflow outright, cool it down after repeated failures, or roll it back to the last good version.

Durable audit log

Every approval and policy decision is recorded and queryable — not scattered across logs.

Durable workflow orchestration

BoundFlow schedules, tracks state, and recovers the whole multi-step workflow — the agent is just one operation inside a process it owns end to end.

Who is it for?

Teams running agents unattended, past the demo stage.

You're running LLM agents in production without a human watching every run — and something has already gone wrong, or you're waiting for it to.

You've hand-coded budget checks, retry limits, or approval flows into your agents, and want them declared as policy instead of scattered exception code.

You need an audit trail of every approval and policy decision your agents make — not something you have to stitch together after an incident.

Architecture

Bring your own inference. Always.

Your agents call Claude with your own Anthropic key, running in your worker. The BoundFlow backend schedules, dispatches, governs, and audits — but it never sees your key or your inference traffic. Your keys, your data, and your token spend stay on your side of the wire.

Your client / SDK invoke · approve · query
gRPC — invoke · approve · query →
Your worker runs agents + tools, with your API key
↔ gRPC stream — launch / result
BoundFlow backend schedules · dispatches · governs · audits

Two independent connections into the same control plane — your client drives and observes workflows, your worker executes them. They never talk to each other directly.
